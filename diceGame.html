Baseball Dice Game

Rules

Pitching

If a 1 is rolled, the pitcher hits the batter, advancing them and any runners one base.
If a 2 or 3 is rolled, the pitch misses the strike zone for a ball.
If the highest possible number is rolled, the pitch is an automatic strike.
With any other result, the pitch is in the strike zone, and it is the batter's turn to roll.

Fastball, Changeup, or Curveball?

The Fastball die is 12 sided, giving a higher strike zone chance, and lower automatic strike and hit batter chances.
The Changeup die is 10 sided, giving a medium strike zone chance, and slightly higher chances for an automatic strike and hit batter compared to the Fastball.
The Curveball die is 8 sided, giving the lowest strike zone chance, and highest chance for automatic strike and hit batsmen.

Batting

If a 1 is rolled, the batter is out.
If a 2 or 3 is rolled, the batter swings and misses.
If the highest possible number is rolled, the batter hits a home run.
Anything else results in hitting the ball.

Power vs Contact

The Power die is 7 sided, meaning a lower hit chance, but higher chance to hit a home run.
The Contact die is 11 sided, giving a higher hit chance, but a lower chance to hit a home run.

Ball in play

When a ball is hit by the batter, a 20 sided die is rolled.

If a 5 or less is rolled, the batter is out.
If a 6-10 is rolled, the ball is hit foul.
If an 11-16 is rolled, the batter hits a single, advancing any runners one base.
If a 17 or 18 is rolled, the batter hits a double, advancing any runners two bases.
If a 19 is rolled, the batter hits a triple, advancing any runners three bases.
If a 20 is rolled, the batter hits a home run, scoring any base runners in addition to the batter.
